# Betty — Product Context

## Register
product

## Product Purpose
Betty is a play-money sportsbook for a single fantasy football league ("Cope & Rebuild,"
10 managers). Sleeper sets the lines (real projections become odds ladders); members stake
a scarce weekly bankroll on player props and parlays. The point isn't the money, it's the
**public bet feed and the season leaderboard** — seeing who's brave, who's an idiot, and
who's actually good. It's a group-chat artifact you can bet inside of.

## Users
The 10 managers of one fantasy league. They already know each other and talk trash daily.
They check Betty on a phone during the week (browsing lines, placing bets) and on a laptop
on Sundays (watching the feed light up as games resolve). Fantasy-literate: they read odds,
projections, and player positions fluently. Nobody needs onboarding about what a parlay is.

## Tone
Cheeky, social, a little degenerate. "betty." is a person, not a corporation, a bit of a
retro bookie. Confident and fun, never the sterile compliance-speak of a real sportsbook.
Trash-talk energy. Wins should feel loud; busts should sting with a wink.

## Strategic Principles
- The **feed is the product**. Lines and the leaderboard orbit it. Everything optimizes for
  "fun to watch other people bet."
- **Glanceable odds.** A manager scanning 200+ markets on a phone must parse price, side,
  and player in under a second. Density with clarity, not minimalism.
- **Real stakes via scarcity, not real money.** Bankroll is finite and doesn't stack; the
  leaderboard is the trophy.
- **You own the line you took.** Frozen odds at bet time. The board is a living market.

## Anti-references
- DraftKings / FanDuel: corporate neon-green-on-black sportsbook chrome, promo-banner
  clutter, responsible-gambling legalese. Betty is the opposite: personal, playful, clean.
- Robinhood-style fintech minimalism: too sterile, too serious about money that isn't real.
- Generic admin-dashboard card grids.

## Surfaces
Single-page board: header (identity + bankroll/PnL), the markets board (filterable,
sortable player props with odds ladders), the live bet feed (SSE), the season leaderboard,
and a floating bet slip (singles + parlays).
